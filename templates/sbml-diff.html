sbml-diff

sbml-diff is a tool for visually representing a single SBML model, or for visually comparing 2 or more SBML models. It can be used in three ways:
  • As a command-line tool
  • As a python package
  • through our web interface
The code is on Github.

Visual Semantics of the Results

Ellipses represent chemical species. Rectangles represent reactions. Parallelograms represent rules.

Directed edges with solid lines join each reactant species to the corresponding reactions, and each reaction to the corresponding product species. Dashed lines join species to reactions whose rate they modify, but in which they are not reactants; the corresponding kineticLaw for the reaction is analysed to determine whether this influence is activatory or inhibitory, and the arrowhead shape is set accordingly.

Rules of type AssigmentRule or RateRule that set concentrations of particular species are represented as parallelograms, with dotted lines indicating directed away from the species they depend on, and towards the species that they affect.

Reactions or rules that appear in more than one model, but with different kinetic laws, are displayed with a grey background. Colouring is used to indicate whether each node and edge is common to all models (grey), some but not all models (black), or a single model (a colour specific to that model).

The user can choose between labelling reaction nodes with the corresponding reaction name, kinetic law, or name and rate law, or leaving them unlabelled.

Limitations

Reactions and species are compared based on their ID; sbml-diff cannot recognise that two reactions in different model files are the same unless they have the same name.

The arrowheads indicating activation/repression of a reaction by a ModifierSpecies may be misleading if the corresponding kineticLaw is not monotonic, or if the kineticLAw could represent either activation and repression, depending on the exact parameter values chosen.
